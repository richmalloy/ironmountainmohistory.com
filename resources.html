The Shelf — Key Publications

Nason, F.L. — A Report on the Iron Ores of Missouri (Missouri Geological Survey, 1892)

The great nineteenth-century survey of the state's iron deposits, with detailed description of the Iron Mountain workings, including the Little Mountain open cut.

Free: digitized on the Internet Archive (archive.org) — search the title.

Crane, G.W. — The Iron Ores of Missouri (Missouri Bureau of Geology & Mines, 2nd Series Vol. X, 1912)

The definitive early-twentieth-century treatment; source of the Big and Little Mountains topographic plate held in the state mine map repository.

Free: digitized in the Missouri DNR/MGS online library at share.mo.gov — search the title.

Hayes, W.C., Jr. — Geology and Exploration of Missouri Iron Deposits (MGS Misc. Publication MP-017, 1959)

By the state geologist whose surname may attach to Hayes Cut; his 1951 doctorate treated Missouri's Precambrian iron deposits.

Free: PDF at share.mo.gov

Hayes, W.C., Jr. (chairman) — Guidebook to the Geology of the St. Francois Mountain Area (MGS Report of Investigations No. 26, October 1961)

Contains Murphy & Mejia's "Underground Geology at Iron Mountain" — the company geologists' own account, the earliest known appearance of "Hayes Cut" in print, and the 1961 surface map naming the Hayes, Big, and Newman cuts.

Digitized on HathiTrust (hathitrust.org) — search the title; page-by-page download available.

Kisvarsanyi, Hebrank & Ryan — Guidebook to the Geology and Ore Deposits of the St. Francois Mountains, Missouri (MGS RI-67, 1981)

The successor guidebook, co-authored by a Hanna Mining Company geologist; Iron Mountain is treated in Road Log No. 2.

Free: PDF at share.mo.gov

Murphy, J.E. & Ohle, E.L. — "The Iron Mountain Mine," in Ore Deposits of the United States 1933–1967 (AIME, 1968)

The closing-era company account of the deposit and mine, by the geologist whose signature appears on the 1961–62 company map series.

Access: OneMine.org (membership) or university & MGS Rolla libraries holding AIME volumes.

Mining and Exploration History of the Southeast Missouri Iron Metallogenic Province

Modern scholarly history of the district, drawing on the Missouri Geological Survey photo archives — including a period photograph of the abandoned Big Cut, early 1900s.

Available via ResearchGate (researchgate.net) — search the title.

Edgar, Judge William R. — "History of Iron Mountain" (local history, ca. 1940s)

The richest narrative account of the property: the Pratte grant, Missouri City, company-town life, the Little Mountain cut, the water system, the Smollinger race track, and the Busby–Hanna revival. The same collection preserves the Lead Belt News account of the September 1923 hoist house fire.

Free: transcribed at RootsWeb — St. Francois County mining history

Miles, J. Tom — Brief Authentic History of St. Francois County (1935)

County history with valuable detail on the 1848–1860 company town: payroll, wages, scrip, and the company hotel.

Available through the State Historical Society of Missouri and county genealogical collections.

Geological Society of America — Memorial to William Clifton Hayes, Jr. (1920–1991)

Biography of Missouri's fourteenth state geologist — candidate namesake of Hayes Cut.

Free: PDF at geosociety.org

The Vaults — Archives & Repositories

Missouri Geological Survey — Mine Map Repository (Rolla)

Holds sixty-three archived sheets of the Iron Mountain Mine, from an 1886 survey by mining engineer W.E. Potter to the Ozark Ore, Hanna Mining, and Midwest Ore working maps of 1961–62 — including the December 1961 "Surface Map Showing Plant Site."

Index: dnrservices.mo.gov — mine maps (township 35N-4E) · High-resolution copies: 573-368-2100

Missouri State Archives — Union Provost Marshal Papers, 1861–1866

Searchable index of more than 72,000 Civil War–era records: loyalty oaths, passes, arrests, and property claims — the place to seek named individuals at Iron Mountain during the federal occupation.

Search: Missouri State Archives provost database (County: Saint Francois)

The War of the Rebellion: Official Records

The government compilation of Union and Confederate records. Series I, Vol. 3 covers the 1861 occupation era; Series I, Vol. 41 (four parts) covers Price's 1864 Missouri Expedition and the Iron Mountain Railroad garrisons.

Free & full-text searchable: Cornell — Making of America

National Park Service — Civil War Battle Units

Unit service records compiled from Dyer's Compendium — source of the documented movement of the 25th Missouri Infantry to Iron Mountain, March 1863.

Free: nps.gov/civilwar — Search Battle Units

State Historical Society of Missouri

Mining company records, manuscript collections, county histories, and the Ramsay Place Name files (1928–45) — the best remaining hope for the origins of "Newman Cut" and "Blue John Cut."

Guide: shsmo.org — mining research guide

Historic aerial photography

USDA/USGS flights of St. Francois County from the 1930s onward show the plant in operation; Google Earth's historical-imagery slider covers recent decades.

USGS EarthExplorer (earthexplorer.usgs.gov) · Google Earth → Historical Imagery

Mindat — Iron Mountain Mine locality

Mineralogical record of the deposit (hematite, magnetite) with historic photographs, including an 1875 illustration and early-1900s mine crew images.

Free: mindat.org — locality 44224

A Caution — Two Look-Alikes

Researchers of this property regularly collide with two unrelated namesakes. The Missouri Mines State Historic Site in Park Hills preserves a lead-mining operation with no connection to Iron Mountain's iron works — its images are frequently mislabeled as Iron Mountain online. And "Camp Iron Mountain" was a Second World War desert training camp in California, sometimes erroneously grafted onto this property's Civil War history. Neither belongs in the Iron Mountain, Missouri record.
